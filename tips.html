Travel Tips for Visiting Taniti

Helpful information to plan with confidence

Taniti is a welcoming and safe destination for visitors. The information below answers many of the most frequently asked questions from first-time travelers and can help you avoid unexpected surprises.

Money & Payments

  • The U.S. dollar is the primary currency on Taniti.
  • Many businesses also accept euros and Japanese yen.
  • Major credit cards are widely accepted.
  • Several banks on the island offer currency exchange services.

Power & Electricity

  • Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.
  • No power adapters are needed for U.S. devices.

Language & Communication

  • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English.
  • English is commonly spoken in tourist areas.
  • Very little English is spoken in rural areas.

Alcohol Laws

  • Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • The legal drinking age is 18.
  • Age restrictions are not strictly enforced.

Health & Medical Care

  • Taniti has one hospital and several medical clinics.
  • The hospital employs multilingual staff.
  • Basic medical care is readily available for visitors.

Safety & Security

  • Violent crime is very rare on Taniti.
  • As tourism increases, petty crimes such as pickpocketing may occur.
  • Keep valuables secure in busy areas.

Holidays & Closures

  • Taniti observes many national holidays.
  • Some attractions and restaurants may close on holidays.
  • Plan activities in advance when visiting during holiday periods.

Local Transportation Notes

  • Helmets are required by law when riding bicycles.
  • Taniti City is flat and very walkable.
  • Taxis are recommended for evening travel.

Most travelers find Taniti easy to navigate and comfortable to visit. A little planning goes a long way toward a smooth and enjoyable trip.
